Wisconsin Native, Marine Corps Veteran, Nature Enthusiast

I am Alex Krebs, and I am the GIS Coordinator for Wisconsin Emergency Management, a position I have had the privilege of serving in since November of 2022.

I was fortunate to travel the world while serving in the United States Marine Corp, where I spent time in Australia, Guam and Japan. After serving 4 years, I left Active Duty Service to pursue my education.

From 2018 to 2022, I attend the University of Wisconsin-Whitewater, where I received a Bachelorâ€™s of Science in Environmental Science, with a Certificate in GIS.

While at UW-Whitewater, I was given an amazing opportunity to conduct undergraduate research, where another student and I sampled over 1,000 trees in southern Wisconsin to collect data on how different tree species reflect and absorb light. We used GIS tools like Survey123 and ArcGIS Pro to record and map field collection. We even presented our research at the 20th Annual UW-System Symposium for Undergraduate Research.

Over the past 2 and a half years, I have been supporting State and Local Emergency Management efforts across Wisconsin as the GIS Coordinator for Wisconsin Emergency Management (WEM).

In this role, I have staffed the State Emergency Operations Center's (SEOC) GIS Role for numerous real-world activations. My primary responsibilities in the SEOC are providing situational awareness and collecting damage locations to help coordinate recovery operations.

I also provide daily support for the sections of WEM, including managing our statewide damage assessment survey using Survey123, supporting GIS needs in various statewide planning efforts and much more!
